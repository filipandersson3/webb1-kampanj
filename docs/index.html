Make the school meals edible in Sweden

All students need energy to do school work, so where are they supposed to get that energy if they can’t get it through their food?

Here are the facts:

  • 40% of students in Sweden think that the school lunch is either bad or really bad.
  • The average cost of one day’s school lunch for one student is 10.47 Swedish Krona.

We need to make sure that the school lunch is both nutritious and that students are willing to eat it.

As of 2020 the school laws in Sweden say that the school meals should be free and nutritious. (Skollag SFS 2010:800) However, if the school meals don’t get eaten by the students because they don’t taste good enough then it doesn’t matter if the food is nutritious or not.

Students who don’t eat the school meals then have to buy some other lunch to replace it which costs money and leads to schools no longer being free. Some students can’t afford that and the school might not even be close enough to any restaurants. We don’t think that students should receive gourmet food at school, as that money could be better spent on other parts of the education system, but we think that it should at least be edible.

We could achieve this by increasing the budget for school lunches or setting up a food council that handles any concerns regarding the quality of the food.

You can help by spreading the message with the hashtag #FixtheFacinorousFood on social media or by helping us organize a strike for better food.

Frequently asked questions

People always have differing opinions about food. Why should I care about school lunches?

Although it is true that people like different kinds of food, if you invited ten guests to your house and four of them didn’t like your cooking, you would probably want to cook something different next time.

Can I donate to this campaign?

We are not currently accepting donations, but you can help by spreading the message.

When is the strike happening?

We are planning a school strike for 23 March 2021 if everything goes as planned. Students attending the strike will gather outside the school for a day to protest for better food. But this will only work if we get enough students to attend the strike, so you need to spread the word.

What are your plans after the strike?

We will evaluate if it worked by sending an anonymous form to all schools in Sweden to compare and see if anything has changed.
